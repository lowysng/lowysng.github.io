March 2021

I don't know exactly what it's like out there, but personally, as a 23 year old person who has thought somewhat deeply about what he wants out of his own short life on this earth, I am not very optimistic about the state of the world as it is and the trajectory it is heading towards. Every now and then, something nice happens that makes the world feel less dreaded and miserable. But I feel that's the exceptional case --- most of the time, things are just naturally bound to go to shit.

Maybe its social media. Every day, I open up my phone and my face gets instantly blasted by threads of someone on the Internet yelling at some other person on the Internet. Or articles that provide zero value to anyone other than their clickbait titles that are designed to misinform and get your blood boiling as quick as possible. Or profiles of people that you probably will never interact with in your life, but nonetheless worship.

Maybe its the corporations. This one bums me out really bad. I don't know how overworking yourself became the norm. I've never had a real job (currently have other plans going on), but I am not looking forward to getting one at all. Whatsapp groups that never shut themselves up? No, thank you. Phone calls from your boss asking you for something while you are out having dinner with your friends? No, thank you. "Being a hard worker is a virtue". Yes, that is correct. But most people never get fairly compensated for their work. Where is the line between hard work and exploitation? Is there even one?

Maybe its the schools. These facilities label themselves as "educational institutes", but if you look closer, you'd start to think, maybe the more appropriate term is "businesses". Most people go to school not to learn how to think but to become sheeps. Graduates that know nothing beyond passing standardized exams. People that spout not what's on their minds but what other people are spouting. "100% graduate employability", "Top 10 in Asia", "Top 100 in the world". Look closer again and you'll see the little asterisks that follow the claims.

Maybe its the pandemic. This one's not that bad. At least it is temporary and not permanent. Still, I think the legacy of COVID should not be about the disease itself but about how it revealed the incompetencies of those that are at the top --- those that are supposed to get things in order and under control. Perhaps I am underestimating the difficulty of the task at hand, but others are dealing with it just fine. What's our excuse?
